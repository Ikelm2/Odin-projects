Description

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish that typically consists of vinegared rice, combined with a variety of ingredients like raw fish, seafood, vegetables, and sometimes tropical fruits. It’s often served with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.

Sushi can be made in various forms, including rolls (maki), pieces of fish on rice (nigiri), or even hand rolls (temaki). It’s known for its delicate flavors, fresh ingredients, and artistic presentation.

Ingredients

  • Sushi rice
  • Nori
  • Fresh fish

  1. Cook the rice: Rinse sushi rice under cold water until clear. Combine rice and water in a rice cooker or pot, and cook according to the rice cooker’s instructions or over medium heat until water is absorbed.
  2. Season the rice: While rice is cooking, combine rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small bowl. Heat until sugar dissolves. Once rice is done, transfer to a bowl and gently stir in the vinegar mixture. Let cool to room temperature.
  3. Prepare the fillings: Slice fresh fish, cucumber, and avocado into thin strips.
  4. Assemble the sushi:
    • Place a nori sheet on a bamboo sushi mat.
    • Spread a thin layer of rice over the nori, leaving about an inch at the top edge.
    • Place the fish, cucumber, and avocado along the bottom edge of the rice.
    • Roll tightly using the sushi mat, then seal the edge with a little water.
  5. Slice and serve: Slice the sushi roll into bite-sized pieces. Serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.
