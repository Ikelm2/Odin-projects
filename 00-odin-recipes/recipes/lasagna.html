lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made of layers of pasta, meat (typically beef or pork), tomato sauce, and cheese, often topped with béchamel sauce or ricotta.The dish is baked until golden and bubbly, resulting in a comforting, hearty meal.

Variations include vegetarian versions with vegetables or different types of cheeses. It’s a popular family favorite and often served in large portions.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef
  • Mozzarella cheese

  1. Cook the noodles: Boil lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare the meat sauce:
    • In a pan, cook ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork) until browned.
    • Add chopped onions and garlic, cook until soft.
    • Stir in tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes. Simmer for 15–20 minutes.
    • Season with salt, pepper, and herbs (like oregano or basil).
  3. Prepare the cheese mixture:
    • In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with an egg, salt, and pepper.
    • Optional: add parmesan or herbs for extra flavor.
  4. Preheat the oven: Set the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  5. Assemble the lasagna:
    • In a baking dish, layer in this order:
      1. A little meat sauce on the bottom
      2. Lasagna noodles
      3. Ricotta cheese mixture
      4. Meat sauce
      5. Mozzarella cheese
    • Repeat layers 2–3 times, finishing with sauce and mozzarella/parmesan on top.
  6. Bake: Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake another 15–20 minutes until bubbly and golden.
  7. Rest and serve: Let the lasagna sit for 10–15 minutes before cutting. This helps it set for easier serving.
